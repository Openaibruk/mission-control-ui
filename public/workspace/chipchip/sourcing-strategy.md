# ChipChip Optimal Sourcing Strategy

**Author:** Nahom (VP Strategy / Marketing)
**Date:** March 19, 2026

Based on the Sourcing Team Drive audit, here is the strategic framework for obtaining products at the best price while maintaining strict quality standards.

## 1. Direct-to-Source (D2S) Aggregation
- **Strategy:** Bypass middleman wholesale markets (e.g., Sunday markets) and increase the "Manufacturer/Farm Direct Purchase" metric to over 70% (currently targeted at 50% in the incentive structure).
- **Implementation:** Consolidate volume purchases directly with large-scale producers, specifically targeting high-loss items like Carrots, Tomatoes, and Red Onions.
- **Outcome:** Lower baseline costs and fresher produce directly from the source, mitigating transit delays.

## 2. Dynamic Predictive Bidding & Contract Lock-ins
- **Strategy:** Transition from reactive price-matching (App Team comparing dispatch notifications against local shops) to a predictive contract model.
- **Implementation:**
  - Lock in "Floor and Ceiling" prices with primary suppliers for 3-month windows to prevent volatility.
  - Require a 48-hour advance notice for supplier bidding rather than pre-dispatch.
- **Outcome:** Secures the lowest possible rate while giving the supplier guaranteed volume, preventing last-minute price gouging.

## 3. Strict QC-Linked Payment Tiers
- **Strategy:** Make quality standards (e.g., Phase 1 Red Onion specs) directly affect supplier payouts.
- **Implementation:**
  - **Grade A (Premium):** 100% of contracted price.
  - **Grade B (Commercial):** 85% of contracted price (automatically discounted if intake QC detects size/weight variations).
  - **Grade C (Processing):** 60% of contracted price (used immediately for flash sales or processing partners).
  - **Rejection Threshold:** Arrival damage > 3% triggers an automatic penalty or full rejection of the batch.
- **Outcome:** Transfers the cost of poor quality back to the supplier, incentivizing better pre-dispatch grading.

## 4. Automated Flash Sale Triggers (Spoilage Mitigation)
- **Strategy:** Automate the Strategic Product Manager's (SPM) manual flash sale decisions.
- **Implementation:** If warehouse damage tracks above 1% or shelf-life exceeds 36 hours (within the 48-hour cycle), automatically reduce the retail price by 15% and push an SMS/App Notification to users.
- **Outcome:** Drastically reduces the total Birr lost to warehouse damage (e.g., 45,000+ Birr/week) by liquidating near-limit stock at cost rather than taking a total loss.

## 5. Incentive Restructuring
- **Strategy:** Double down on the current FMCG/Fresh incentive structure.
- **Implementation:** Increase the bonus multiplier for "Price Difference (Market vs ChipChip)" when sourcing achieves >20% below market average. Add a specific KPI for "Zero Warehouse Rejections" to reward buyers who consistently source high-quality produce.
- **Outcome:** Drives the sourcing team to relentlessly hunt for the best price-to-quality ratio.

## Summary
By enforcing **Direct-to-Source purchasing**, linking **payouts to strict QC grades**, and automating **flash sales for high-risk inventory**, ChipChip can dramatically reduce the ~10% combined arrival/warehouse damage loss while securing products at 15-20% below market rates.
