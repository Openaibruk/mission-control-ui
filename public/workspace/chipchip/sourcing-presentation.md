# 🚀 Optimal Sourcing Strategy
## Balancing Best Price & Quality Standards
**ChipChip Sourcing Team**
March 2026

---

# 📊 1. Direct-to-Source (D2S) Aggregation
**Bypass middleman wholesale markets**
- Increase "Manufacturer/Farm Direct Purchase" metric to >70%.
- Consolidate volume purchases directly with large-scale producers.
- Specifically target high-loss items like Carrots, Tomatoes, and Red Onions.
- **Outcome:** Lower baseline costs and fresher produce directly from the source, mitigating transit delays.

---

# 🔐 2. Predictive Bidding & Contract Lock-ins
**Transition from reactive price-matching to a predictive model**
- Lock in "Floor and Ceiling" prices with primary suppliers for 3-month windows.
- Require a 48-hour advance notice for supplier bidding rather than pre-dispatch.
- Benchmarking should happen *before* the harvest season for key goods.
- **Outcome:** Secures the lowest possible rate while giving the supplier guaranteed volume, preventing last-minute price gouging.

---

# 📈 3. Strict QC-Linked Payment Tiers
**Make quality standards directly affect supplier payouts**
- **Grade A (Premium):** 100% of contracted price.
- **Grade B (Commercial):** 85% of contracted price (discounted if intake QC detects size/weight variations).
- **Grade C (Processing):** 60% of contracted price (used immediately for flash sales or processing partners).
- **Rejection Threshold:** Arrival damage > 3% triggers an automatic penalty or full rejection.

---

# ⚡ 4. Automated Flash Sale Triggers
**Automate manual flash sale decisions to stop spoilage leaks**
- **Trigger:** If warehouse damage tracks above 1% or shelf-life exceeds 36 hours.
- **Action:** Automatically reduce the retail price by 15% and push an SMS/App Notification to users.
- **Impact:** Drastically reduces total Birr lost to warehouse damage (e.g., stopping the 45,000+ Birr/week loss observed in carrots/tomatoes).

---

# 💰 5. Incentive Restructuring
**Double down on the FMCG/Fresh incentive structure**
- Increase the bonus multiplier for "Price Difference (Market vs ChipChip)" when sourcing achieves >20% below market average.
- Add a specific KPI for **"Zero Warehouse Rejections"**.
- **Outcome:** Drives the sourcing team to relentlessly hunt for the best price-to-quality ratio.

---

# 🏁 Summary
By enforcing **Direct-to-Source purchasing**, linking **payouts to strict QC grades**, and automating **flash sales for high-risk inventory**, ChipChip can dramatically reduce the ~10% combined arrival/warehouse damage loss while securing products at 15-20% below market rates.
