# ChipChip Sourcing Team Audit Report

**Date:** March 19, 2026
**Source:** Sourcing Team Google Drive (ID: `1N_7-v0JhRToQgiwi5PR2NqHGL4PfB96s`)

## 1. Current Sourcing Practices & Model
- **Consignment Marketplace Model:** ChipChip operates a consignment model where inventory ownership remains with the supplier until the product is sold to the final customer.
- **Supplier Responsibilities:** Suppliers are responsible for the transport of goods to the Addis warehouse and bear the transport damage risk.
- **Bidding & Dispatch Process:** Before dispatching goods, suppliers must notify the App Team with quantity (KG), grade expectation, base price, and expected dispatch date. The App Team benchmarks this against other suppliers, Sunday market prices, local shop prices, and historical trends. Dispatch is only confirmed if the supplier's price is competitive; otherwise, a discount is requested or alternative suppliers are used.
- **Stock & Spoilage Management:** A 48-hour delivery cycle is targeted. The Strategic Product Manager (SPM) proactively adjusts prices and launches flash sales to move stock at risk of spoilage.

## 2. Pricing & Margin Controls
- **Dynamic Pricing:** Selling prices are adjusted daily based on expected replacement costs, farm prices, DC prices, and market benchmarks to protect margins.
- **Incentive Structures:** Performance bonuses and incentives for the FMCG/Fresh teams are heavily tied to:
  - Total sales and gross margin targets.
  - Sourcing products below market average (e.g., higher incentive for 10-20% price down vs. market).
  - Manufacturer direct purchases (target: 50%).
  - Penalties are applied for unpicked orders.
- **Run Rate Tracking:** Weekly tracking measures total purchases, sales against targets, and calculates the average run rate per day (e.g., ~5,000-6,000 Birr/day in recent metrics).

## 3. Quality Control (QC) & Standards
- **Strict Intake Recording:** Upon arrival at the warehouse, QC records total KG received, arrival damage percentage, and grade breakdown.
- **Damage Tracking:** Both "Arrival Damage" and "Warehouse Damage" are rigorously tracked in Kg, Birr, and Percentage (e.g., recent weeks show arrival damage at 0.67%–4.4% and warehouse damage at 0.62%–7.8%). 
- **Product-Specific Losses:** The tracking highlights specific items with high loss rates (e.g., Carrots, Tomatoes, and Red Onions frequently showing high warehouse damage).
- **Grading Specifications:** Clear, phased grading protocols exist for products. For example, *Premium Grade A Red Onions* must have a diameter > 7.0 cm, weight between 200g-350g, deep purple glossy color, extremely firm texture, and zero mechanical/fungal damage. Commercial Grade B and Processing Grade C have lower thresholds.

## 4. Key Takeaways & Gaps
- **Data-Driven Sourcing:** The team uses robust weekly trackers for sales, targets, and damage, but relies heavily on manual benchmarking before dispatch.
- **Loss Reduction Opportunity:** Warehouse damage on perishables like carrots and tomatoes represents a significant financial leak (e.g., 45,000+ Birr loss in a single week). The focus should be on tightening the cold chain or accelerating the flash sale trigger for these specific items.
- **Incentive Alignment:** The incentive system effectively aligns buyer behavior with company goals (direct manufacturer purchasing, beating market price, driving volume).
