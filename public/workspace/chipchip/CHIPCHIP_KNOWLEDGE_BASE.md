## Executive Summary

### What is ChipChip?

ChipChip is an **asset-light food trade infrastructure platform** based in **Addis Ababa, Ethiopia**. It connects farmers/suppliers with consumers, restaurants, and hotels through a distributed network of **Super Leaders** (community micro-distributors) and **B2B Captains** (restaurant supply coordinators).

### Core Vision

ChipChip evolves from a simple marketplace into a **distributed food trade infrastructure** built on three pillars:
- **Demand Network** — Aggregating demand across consumer groups, Super Leaders, and B2B buyers
- **Supply Marketplace** — Connecting farmers, cooperatives, and wholesalers with demand
- **Trust & Data Infrastructure** — Building reputation networks, credit scoring, and open data layers

The platform becomes the **operating system of local food trade**.

### The 2026 Reset

As of early 2026, ChipChip is executing a strategic reset called **"CHIPCHIP RESET 2026"** — a deliberate shift from chaotic, loss-making growth to **disciplined, sustainable scale**.

**Key principles of the reset:**
- **Discipline First** — Technology and process exist to enforce discipline, not enable chaos
- **Volume Quality** — Bad volume is worse than no volume. Quality > Quantity
- **One Owner** — One KPI, One Owner. If two teams own it, no one owns it

### Mission

Stop touching products and start **controlling flows**. ChipChip becomes:
- Demand aggregator
- Flow designer
- Settlement engine
- Risk controller
- SLA enforcer

### End-State Vision (Month 12)

- **0% B2C Volume** (migrated to aggregation)
- **100% B2B + B2B2C Mix** (profitable, predictable)
- ChipChip as the **"Veins of the Economy"** — profitable, predictable distribution

## Strategy

### Asset-Light Operating Model

**Traditional model:** Farm → Warehouse → Distribution → Retail  
**ChipChip model:** Farm/Supplier → Super Leaders → Consumers (B2B2C)  
**B2B model:** Supplier → B2B Captain → Restaurants

ChipChip coordinates (demand aggregation, supplier matching, payments, logistics) **without owning physical infrastructure**.

### 3-Layer Asset-Light Flow

```
Farm → Micro-DC (Supplier-owned or 3PL) → Super Leader (B2B cluster) → Direct Restaurant (B2B bulk)
```

#### Flow 1: Super Leader Direct Cluster (B2B2C)
- Farm → Aggregator/Supplier DC → Super Leader → B2C Pickup
- No central warehouse
- Super Leaders commit volume weekly (forecast-based)
- Supplier delivers boxed SKUs to SL hubs
- ChipChip: Controls pricing, digital ordering, settlement, takes margin
- Result: No warehouse pick & pack, no last-mile fragmentation, higher route density

#### Flow 2: Direct DC → Restaurants (B2B)
- Supplier/Aggregator DC → Restaurant
- No warehouse touch unless QC escalation or FMCG bundling
- Restaurants order by 7PM, supplier fulfills next morning
- ChipChip: Aggregates orders, sends consolidated PO, tracks SLA, manages payment & BNPL

### Key Design Principle

> "Push physical risk outward. Keep digital + rule control inward."

ChipChip currently carries: supplier risk, handling risk, storage risk, delivery risk, damage risk, credit risk.

Asset-light means keeping only: demand risk, credit risk, reputation risk.

### 5 Non-Negotiable Rules (Reset 2026)

| # | Rule | Description |
|---|------|-------------|
| 1 | **Ownership** | One KPI, One single owner. If two teams own it, no one owns it. |
| 2 | **Enforcement** | Systems enforce rules via Blocks and Alerts. |
| 3 | **Priority** | Operations and Finance always come first in every decision. |
| 4 | **Technology** | Tech builds enforcement, not "nice-to-have" shiny tools. |
| 5 | **Escalation** | Automatic system escalation, never personal or subjective. |

### New Non-Negotiable Rules (Asset-Light Model)

1. **No Same-Day Flexibility** — Everything must have cutoff. No warehouse buffer = no forgiveness.
2. **Volume Commitment Windows** — Super Leaders and Restaurants must commit volume and lock orders.
3. **Supplier Liability Shift** — Damage/late/wrong quantity: Supplier absorbs first layer.
4. **CP1 Auto-Block** — No order below margin floor. System-enforced.

### Packaging System

#### Bag System (Deposit/Refund)
- Big bag: 30 ETB deposit
- Mid bag: 15 ETB deposit
- Refund becomes "Packaging Credit" (auto-used on next order)
- Refund only after warehouse confirmation (not at driver stage)

#### Crate System (Asset Control)
- Crates tracked with Asset ID (QR/barcode)
- Driver crate liability ledger (trip-based)
- End-of-day reconciliation mandatory
- No trip allowed next day until resolved

#### Hybrid Model
- **B2B:** Full crate exchange system, mandatory exchange, no return → next delivery blocked
- **Super Leaders:** Crate pool model, SL responsible, deposit at SL level
- **Direct B2C:** Bag deposit model, encourage migration to SL

### Quality Control — Red Onion Example (Product Standards)

**Grade A (Premium):** Size >7cm, Weight 200-350g, Color Deep Purple, Firm, Zero damage  
**Grade B (Commercial):** Size 5-6.9cm, Weight 150-200g, Minor skin scratches  
**Grade C (Processing):** Size <5cm or >9cm, Weight <150g, Noticeable skin peeling

**Bulk Acceptability:**
- A Batch: ≥80% Grade A, ≤5% Grade C, ≤3% Damaged
- B Batch: ≥80% Grade B, ≤5% Damaged

### Warehouse Operations
- Inbound grading & QC at arrival
- FIFO stock rotation
- Batch ID tracking
- Physical separation of consignment stock
- Stock accuracy tracked as KPI

## KPIs & Reporting

### Weekly KPI Meeting SOP

- **When:** Every Friday
- **Duration:** 2 hours
- **Owner:** Chief of Staff
- **Purpose:** Enforce decisions, protect CP1/CP2, stop KPI drift
- **Core principle:** KPI meetings are for **decisions, not explanations**

### Meeting Structure

#### Block 1: Operator KPI Review (60 min)
- Participants: Team Leads only (Finance, Ops, Supply, Growth, Marketing, Tech)
- Executives: NOT allowed
- Flow:
  1. KPI Health Scan (20 min) — Each KPI labeled 🟢/🟡/🔴, max 60 seconds per KPI
  2. Red KPI Resolution (30 min) — Root cause, proposed action, owner, expected impact
  3. Exec Pack Prep (10 min) — Max 8 slides, only 🔴 KPIs + decisions needed

#### Block 2: Executive KPI Review (45 min)
- Participants: Executives + Finance + relevant Team Leads
- Flow:
  1. Executive Snapshot (10 min) — CP1, CP2, cash position
  2. Red KPI Decisions (25 min) — Approve/Block/Modify
  3. Veto & Freeze (10 min) — Freeze channels, block discounts, stop routes

#### Block 3: Decision Lock (15 min)
- Output: Action list with owner + deadline (sent within 30 minutes after meeting)

### Status Rules
- 🟢 = On target / improving
- 🟡 = Off target, corrective action active
- 🔴 = Off target, no credible fix
- 🔴 KPI for 2 consecutive weeks → automatic escalation

### Week Range
- Thursday → Wednesday (for KPI tracking)

## Contracts & Agreements

### Consignment Sales & Operations Agreement (Supplier Model)

**Key terms:**
- **Model:** Consignment Marketplace — inventory ownership remains with Supplier until sold
- **ChipChip provides:** App store profile, marketing, warehousing, fulfillment, delivery, settlement
- **Pricing:** Supplier base price + ChipChip adds 5-10% margin
- **Settlement:** Batch-based. Total Sold KG × Selling Price – ChipChip Margin = Net Payable
- **Payment:** After delivery completion and batch closure

**Damage Responsibility Matrix:**

| Damage Type | Responsible |
|-------------|-------------|
| Farm quality | Supplier |
| Transport to warehouse | Supplier |
| Arrival reject | Supplier |
| Edible discount | Supplier |
| Warehouse mishandling | ChipChip |
| Fulfillment delay | ChipChip |
| Last-mile damage | Logistics |
| Expiry due to pricing refusal | Supplier |
| Expiry due to operational failure | ChipChip |

**Key accountability rules:**
- **Packing:** Supplier must pack per restaurant, label clearly, internal QC by 5:30 AM

- **Driver:** Verifies KG, matches dispatch sheet, reports shortage immediately
- **Compensation:** Supplier failures include lost margin + extra KG compensation
- **5 Warnings → Replacement:** After 5 supplier warnings, replacement process starts

### Channel Rules

| **Telegram** | Tasks, updates, alerts | Brainstorming, strategy debates, voice notes |
| **Excel/Sheets** | KPIs, financial tracking, planning tables | — |

| **Calendar** | Time & commitments | — |
| **Dashboards** | Numbers & performance | — |

### The 60-Second Rule
If a message cannot be understood and acted upon in **under 60 seconds**, it does not belong in Telegram.

### Mandatory Decision Format
Every decision shared must include 3 elements:
1. **What is decided**
2. **Who owns it**
3. **By when it must be executed**

If any element is missing, the decision is **invalid**.

### What Is Strictly Prohibited
- Operational debates in announcement groups
- Decisions made in private chats
- Re-opening closed decisions without new data
- Using multiple tools for the same purpose
- Emotional or accusatory language
