## Executive Summary

ChipChip is in the middle of its most consequential strategic transformation since founding. Q1 2026 has been defined by a deliberate pivot from an **asset-heavy B2C grocery delivery company** to an **asset-light food trade infrastructure platform** — what the team calls the "control tower" model.

The numbers tell a story of aggressive restructuring with meaningful but incomplete results:

| Indicator | Status | Signal |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Revenue | 7.4M ETB/month (Feb) | Growing but below 12M target |
| Gross Margin | -16.68% (Feb, improved from -45.49% in Jan) | Trending right, still negative |
| Monthly Burn | 10.16M ETB (Feb, down 48% from Jan) | Dramatic improvement, still unsustainable |
| Warehouse Damage | Down 92% in 6 weeks (7.88% → 0.62%) | Transformation working |
| SGL Channel | Up 389% in 6 weeks | Strategy validated |
| B2B Channel | Collapsed then recovering; 16% of revenue from <1% of orders | Highest ROI channel, underbuilt |
| Daily Run Rate | Peaked at 5,997 ETB (W5), fell to 5,419 (W6) | Momentum fading — needs attention |

**The bottom line:** ChipChip has proven that the asset-light model works operationally — warehouse and arrival damage have collapsed, the SGL channel is surging, and burn has nearly halved. But **the revenue engine is not yet self-sustaining.** The company is still losing ~2.7M ETB/month against 7.4M ETB in sales, with gross margin at -16.68%. With the current runway and burn rate, Q2 2026 is the make-or-break quarter.

Three priorities dominate the path forward:
1. **Achieve CP1 ≥ +5%** through strict margin enforcement and B2B migration
2. **Launch BNPL** to unlock the planned 35% revenue stream
3. **Accelerate SGL network** from ~35 active to 100 to build the distribution moat

## Company Vision & Positioning

### What ChipChip Is Becoming

ChipChip is building **Ethiopia's first asset-light food trade infrastructure platform**. The positioning has undergone a fundamental reframing:

| From (Old Identity) | To (New Identity) |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Warehouse operator | Asset-light flow designer |
| B2C delivery company | Multi-sided marketplace |
| Physical handler | Digital control tower |
| Negotiation-driven pricing | Algorithmic pricing discipline |
| Speculative inventory | Just-in-time, demand-backed sourcing |
| Manual operations | Automated enforcement systems |

**Core Motto:** *"We don't touch products. We control flows."*

### North Star Metric

**Monthly Active Orderers (MAO)** across profitable channels only (B2B + B2B2C). The end-state target is **0% direct B2C volume; 100% B2B + B2B2C mix** through Smart Souks and B2B Captains.

### The 5-Platform Architecture

ChipChip's technical architecture has five interconnected layers:

1. **Platform 1 — Supply SaaS:** Farmers, manufacturers, importers, coops, and DCs post supply lots, track QC scores, and receive escrow-linked settlement.
2. **Platform 2 — Aggregator Marketplace:** Smart Souks (B2C) and B2B Captains (HORECA) book supplier lots, manage storefronts, and earn commissions + bonuses.
3. **Platform 3 — Consumer Marketplace:** B2C households and HORECA customers browse, select nearby Smart Souks, and choose free pickup or paid (3 ETB) home delivery.
4. **Platform 4 — Logistics Layer:** 3PL partners (Eshi, Yango, Beu) handle last-mile execution with proof of delivery and SLA compliance.
5. **Platform 5 — ChipChip Core:** The central brain managing trust, escrow, AI route orchestration, reliability scoring, and revenue routing.

**Master Interaction Principle:** *"Consumer sees aggregator. Aggregator sees supplier. ChipChip sees everything."*

### Market Context

- Ethiopia has 120M+ people, 80%+ rural, with food supply chains dominated by fragmented middlemen
- Addis Ababa alone: 5M+ consumers, thousands of restaurants
- Middlemen add 30-50% markup between farm gate and consumer plate
- Mobile penetration growing rapidly; digital payment adoption still early
- Physical infrastructure (cold chain, warehousing, logistics) underdeveloped
- Food inflation is a primary consumer pain point — pricing advantage matters

## Business Model Deep Dive

### Revenue Streams (Current vs. Target)

| Stream | Current Share | Target Share | Mechanism | Status |
|--------|--------------|-------------|-----------|--------|
| **Trade Margin** | ~95% (de facto) | ~50% | Spread between supplier buy price and platform sell price | **Active** |
| **Financing / BNPL** | 0% | ~35% | Credit products for SGLs, restaurants, suppliers, drivers (7-14 day cycles) | **Not yet launched** |
| **Platform Tools / SaaS** | ~0% | ~15% | Supplier-facing tools, price benchmarking, demand forecasts, marketplace ads | **Planning stage** |
| **Logistics Fees** | ~0% | ~15% | Coordinated routing fees from 3PL orchestration | **Planning stage** |

**Critical gap:** Revenue is almost entirely dependent on trade margin today, while the strategy envisions a diversified four-bucket model where only 50% comes from trade. Until BNPL and SaaS tools launch, margin sustainability hinges entirely on trade economics improving.

### Unit Economics (Per-KG Spread)

| Component | Amount (ETB/kg) |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Supplier buy price | 40 |
| Platform sell price | 46 |
| **Total gross spread** | **6** |
| → Leader / Captain commission | 2–3 |
| → **ChipChip net share** | **~2** |
| → Operations buffer | ~1 |

At ~2 ETB/kg net share, and with 153,989 kg moved in February, platform-level trade margin equals roughly **308,000 ETB** — far below what's needed to cover operating costs. This confirms why volume, B2B higher-margin orders, and supplementary revenue streams are essential.

### Gate Pricing Model — Behavioral Economics as Strategy

ChipChip uses pricing as a mechanism to drive desired behaviors, not just to set prices:

| Segment | Price Logic | Delivery Fee | Intent |
|---------|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Individual B2C | +25% markup | 150 ETB | **Discourage** default B2C |
| Group Member | -15% discount | 10 ETB | **Incentivize** aggregation |
| High-Volume (5kg+) | Tiered discount | FREE (1x/day) | **Reward** basket size |
| Super Groups (50kg+) | Bulk discount | FREE | **Maximize** drop efficiency |
| Bulk Booster | -5%+ discount | Free single drop | **Drive** high-tonnage |

**The golden rule:** *"ChipChip does not negotiate demand. ChipChip prices demand into discipline."*

### Margin Tracking Framework

- **CP1 (Contribution Profit 1):** Gross Margin = Sales − COGS. This is the product-level profitability measure.
- **CP2 (Contribution Profit 2):** Net Margin = Sales − COGS − Warehouse − Delivery. This is channel-level profitability including fulfillment costs.

## Conclusion

ChipChip's Q1 2026 has been a quarter of **radical but necessary surgery**. The company dismantled its own warehouse-centric model and proved that the alternative works: warehouse damage fell 92%, the SGL channel grew 389%, and monthly burn dropped 48%. These are not incremental improvements — they are order-of-magnitude shifts in operational efficiency.

But efficiency gains alone do not create a sustainable business. The critical gaps remain:

1. **Gross margin is still negative** at -16.68%. The company loses money on every birr of revenue until CP1 turns positive.
2. **Revenue is 38% below target** at 7.4M ETB vs. the 12M ETB goal.
3. **The highest-margin channel (B2B) remains underbuilt** at 16% of revenue despite being 60x more efficient per order.
4. **The planned 35% revenue stream (BNPL) has not yet launched.**
5. **Data infrastructure is broken** — no real-time analytics, missing data weeks, no CRM.

**Q2 2026 is the decisive quarter.** The restructuring work done in Q1 created the platform. Q2 must be about commercialization: enforcing margin discipline, scaling the channels that work (SGL, B2B), launching the revenue streams that are planned (BNPL, FMCG), and building the systems that make it all repeatable without manual heroics.

The strategy is sound. The direction is correct. The execution speed and financial discipline over the next 90 days will determine whether ChipChip is the company that redefined Ethiopia's food supply chain — or another cautionary tale of great strategy meeting imperfect execution.

**The clock is ticking. The runway is finite. Focus is the only luxury left.**
