**Period:** Apr 2026 – Apr 2027 (13 months)

## Executive Summary

To execute a credit plan with **89.3M ETB revenue**, **45M ETB total credit exposure**, and **17.97% blended margins** across three revenue streams, ChipChip needs a **scaled team of approximately 28-38 people** by Month 13. This document outlines the team structure, hiring timeline, skill requirements, and operational readiness gaps specific to the Ethiopian B2B credit market.

**The critical insight:** Collections and credit management capacity must scale *ahead* of revenue growth, not behind it. With credit exposure rising from 31% to 63% of revenue, a lean credit team in Month 1 becomes a major risk by Month 8.

## 2. Functional Breakdown

### 2.1 Sales & Account Management

**Role:** Drive revenue across B2B (51.3% of revenue), Super Leader (15.4%), and FMCG (33.3%) channels.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Headcount** | 2 → 6 → 10 |
| **Structure** | 1 B2B Lead + 1 FMCG/SL Account Manager (Month 1); add 2 B2B reps, 1 FMCG rep, 1 SL rep (Month 7); scale to 6 B2B, 2 FMCG, 2 SL (Month 13) |
| **Key Responsibility** | Acquire new merchants, manage relationships, negotiate credit terms, hit monthly revenue targets |
| **Ratio** | 1 sales rep per ~750K–900K ETB monthly revenue at steady state |

**Skills Required:**
- B2B sales experience in Ethiopian wholesale/distribution markets (Merkato, Shola, regional hubs)
- Understanding of credit terms and trade finance — reps must know what "80% credit" means for their customer
- Amharic fluency + regional language (Oromiffa for expansion outside Addis)
- CRM proficiency and pipeline management
- Relationship-driven selling (Ethiopian business culture prioritizes personal trust over transactional agreements)

**Key Risk:** Ethiopian B2B buyers are relationship-driven. Without experienced reps who know the market, merchant acquisition will lag revenue targets.

---

### 2.2 Credit Management & Risk

**Role:** Assess creditworthiness, set limits, monitor portfolio health, and manage credit policy changes (especially Super Leader jumps: 20% → 40% → 80%).

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Headcount** | 1 → 4 → 7 |
| **Structure** | 1 Credit Risk Lead (Month 1); add 1 analyst, 1 underwriter, 1 portfolio manager (Month 7); scale to 4 analysts + 2 underwriters + 1 lead (Month 13) |
| **Key Responsibility** | Credit scoring, limit setting, policy enforcement, portfolio monitoring, NPL tracking |
| **Ratio** | 1 credit officer per ~1.5M–2.0M ETB outstanding credit portfolio |

**Skills Required:**
- Commercial credit analysis experience (banking, MFI, or fintech background)
- Familiarity with Ethiopian credit risk norms (NPL benchmarks, collateral culture)
- Analytical/statistical skills for credit scoring model development
- Understanding of regulatory environment (National Bank of Ethiopia guidelines on credit provision)
- Data analysis — Excel, SQL, or basic BI tools

**Key Risk:** The Super Leader credit rate quadruples from 20% to 80%. The current model assumes 1 credit person in Month 1 — **insufficient even for pilot scale.** This is the **#1 operational readiness gap.** Recommend hiring the Credit Risk Lead **4 weeks before launch** (not at launch).

---

### 2.3 Collections

**Role:** Recover outstanding credits, manage overdue accounts, enforce payment terms.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Headcount** | 1 → 3 → 6 |
| **Structure** | 1 Collections Officer (Month 1); add 2 officers (urban + field) (Month 7); scale to 4 urban + 2 regional (Month 13) |
| **Key Responsibility** | Payment follow-up, escalation management, field visits for delinquent accounts, reporting |
| **Ratio** | 1 collector per ~500K–750K ETB outstanding receivables |

**Skills Required:**
- Collections orAccounts Receivable experience — banking or telecom collections preferred
- Knowledge of Ethiopian debt collection practices and legal framework
- Negotiation and conflict resolution skills
- Firm but culturally appropriate approach — Ethiopian business relationships are long-term; aggressive collections can permanently destroy a merchant relationship
- Amharic + local language proficiency
- Basic digital literacy (mobile payment tracking, CRM data entry)

**Key Risk:** At 2.4% B2B credit margin, a 30-day payment delay represents a 24% annualized loss on that exposure. Collections must be proactive, not reactive. The **first Collections Officer must be hired simultaneously with the Credit Risk Lead.**

---

### 2.4 Operations & Fulfillment

**Role:** Manage order processing, logistics, inventory, warehouse operations, and delivery.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Headcount** | 3 → 6 → 9 |
| **Structure** | 1 Ops Lead + 1 Warehouse + 1 Logistics/Dispatch (Month 1); add 1 Warehouse, 1 Dispatch, 1 Regional Coordinator (Month 7); scale to 3 Warehouse, 2 Dispatch, 2 Regional, 1 Ops Lead + 1 Deputy (Month 13) |
| **Key Responsibility** | Order accuracy, on-time delivery, inventory management, quality control |

**Skills Required:**
- Supply chain / logistics management experience in Ethiopia
- Fleet management (if using own delivery vehicles) or third-party logistics coordination
- Inventory management systems
- Quality assurance — especially for perishable FMCG goods
- Regional market knowledge (delivery routes, market access in Addis and expansion cities)

**Key Risk:** FMCG represents 33.3% of revenue and is subject to step-growth. Order volume will surge 50%+ in specific months — the operations team must be flex-staffed or pre-trained for these spikes.

### 2.5 Technology & Product

**Role:** Build, maintain, and scale the digital credit/BNPL platform, customer app, and admin systems.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Headcount** | 3 → 4 → 4 |
| **Structure** | 1 Tech Lead + 1 Backend Dev + 1 Mobile Dev (Month 1); add 1 QA/Data Analyst (Month 4); maintain through Month 13 |
| **Key Responsibility** | Credit scoring engine, customer app, admin dashboard, integrations, data infrastructure |

**Skills Required:**
- Full-stack development (React/React Native or Flutter for mobile; Node.js/Python backend)
- Supabase or PostgreSQL database management
- API development and third-party integrations (payment gateways, ERP systems)
- Data analytics and dashboard building
- Security best practices (data protection, financial transaction security)
- Ethiopian mobile payment integration experience (Telebirr, CBE Birr, M-Pesa)

**Key Risk:** A 3-person tech team must deliver a credit scoring engine, customer app, admin dashboard, collection system, and integrations within the launch window. This is **highly aggressive.** Recommend either contracting for the credit scoring engine or prioritizing MVP features only.

### 2.6 Finance & Accounting

**Role:** Manage financial reporting, working capital tracking, margin analysis, and compliance.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Headcount** | 1 → 2 → 3 |
| **Structure** | 1 Finance Lead/Accountant (Month 1); add 1 Junior Accountant (Month 7); add 1 Financial Analyst (Month 13) |
| **Key Responsibility** | Financial reporting, margin tracking, working capital management, tax compliance, investor reporting |

**Skills Required:**

- Experience with revenue recognition for credit-based business models
- Working capital management
- Tax compliance (Ethiopian Revenue & Customs Authority requirements)

- ERP/accounting software proficiency

**Key Risk:** The current financial model has **no operating expense tracking** and **no NPL provisions.** The Finance Lead's first priority must be building a complete P&L that includes staffing costs, default provisions, and working capital financing costs.

## 5. Operational Readiness Gaps

### Gap 1: No Credit Management Capacity at Launch
**Severity: 🔴 Critical**
- Plan shows Month 1 credit exposure of 812K ETB (31.1% of revenue)
- Without a Credit Risk Lead hired *before* launch, credit decisions will be ad hoc
- **Action:** Hire Credit Risk Lead 4-6 weeks before launch. Build credit scoring framework during pre-launch.

### Gap 2: No Default/NPL Provision
**Severity: 🔴 Critical**
- Financial model assumes 100% collection on 45M ETB in credits
- Ethiopian B2B NPL rates are 3-8%; a 5% default = 2.25M ETB loss
- **Action:** Finance Lead must build NPL provision into financial model. Budget for 3-5% default reserve.

### Gap 3: No Operating Expense Model
**Severity: 🟠 High**
- 16M ETB margin is meaningful only after operating costs
- At ~39 people by Month 13, salary costs in Addis could consume 4-6M ETB/month
- **Action:** Build full OPEX model in Month 1. Recalculate net profitability.

### Gap 4: Super Leader Credit Jumps Without Risk Capacity
**Severity: 🟠 High**
- Credit rate goes 20% → 40% → 80% for Super Leader channel
- This is a 4x increase in risk exposure with no described mechanism for risk assessment adjustment
- **Action:** Credit team must build dynamic scoring models before each jump. No policy changes without risk team sign-off.

### Gap 5: FMCG Step-Growth Without Operational Buffer
**Severity: 🟡 Medium**
- FMCG grows in discrete jumps (50% increases) rather than smoothly
- Operations must pre-position for these spikes — warehouse space, staffing, delivery capacity
- **Action:** Build flex-staffing plan. Pre-negotiate temporary warehouse/logistics capacity during growth months.

### Gap 6: No Regional Language Coverage
**Severity: 🟡 Medium**
- As operations expand beyond Addis Ababa, Amharic-only teams will face limitations
- **Action:** Prioritize Oromiffa-speaking hires for Dire Dawa and Eastern Ethiopia expansion; Amharic remains sufficient for Addis pilot.

## 8. Summary & Key Recommendations

1. **Hire Credit Risk Lead ASAP — before launch.** This is the single most important hire for the plan's viability.
2. **Build a lean, automation-heavy team first.** Don't hire linearly with revenue — hire ahead of risk, automate routine tasks.
3. **Budget 6-8.5M ETB for payroll over 13 months.** This consumes 37-53% of margin before any other costs.
4. **Create cross-functional risk committee.** Credit + Collections + Sales Lead should meet weekly on portfolio health.
5. **Don't execute Super Leader credit jumps without risk readiness.** Each policy change (20%→40%→80%) must be preceded by 4 weeks of preparation and stress testing.
6. **Plan for bilingual (Amharic + Oromiffa) field staff** as regional expansion begins.
7. **Build the full OPEX model in Month 1** — the current 16M ETB margin is incomplete without costs.
