# Ethiopian Kids Animation — Outcome Summary

## Research Insights (2025)
- AI tools now enable small teams to produce weekly content: script → voice → animation → edit in ~90 min per Short.
- Successful channels (Cocomelon, Gracie’s Corner) use simple character design, repetitive music, and high watch-time loops.
- YouTube Shorts drive discovery; long-form builds monetization watch-hours.
- Localization (Amharic + English) captures both Ethiopia and diaspora.
- 3D animation offers higher reusability and brand IP potential.

## Implementation Plan

### Phase 1: Identity & Scripts (Week 1–2)
- Channel name: “Tibeb Kids” (or “Addis Animations”)
- Characters: 3 core (lion cub, tortoise, school girl in habesha dress)
- 5 pilot scripts: phonics (3), numbers (2) with Amharic keywords

### Phase 2: Pipeline (Week 3–4)
- Rig characters in Blender; AI lip sync (Rhubarb)
- TTS: Google Cloud `am-ET` Wavenet
- Music: Soundraw loops
- Render: Eevee, 1080x1920, 30fps

### Phase 3: Launch (Week 5–8)
- 3 Shorts/week + 1 long-form/month
- SEO: bilingual titles, playlists
- Compliance: Made for Kids (no comments, no personalized ads)
- Promotion: Telegram/FB parent groups, Ethiopian educators

### Phase 4: Scale (Month 3+)
- AdSense + merch (printables)
- Dub into Oromo/Tigrinya
- Explore licensing with ETV, Safaricom Ethiopia
