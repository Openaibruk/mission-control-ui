## Resolution Workflow

### Step 1: Acknowledge (within SLA)

- **Low/Medium:** Within 4 business hours
- **High:** Within 1 hour
- **Critical:** Within 15 minutes

**Acknowledgment Script:**

> Hi [Customer Name],
>
> Thank you for reaching out about [brief description of issue]. I'm [Agent Name], and I'll be handling your case personally.
>
> I've logged your complaint under ticket #[ID] and I'm looking into this right now. I'll have an update for you within [timeframe].
>
> We take this seriously, and I want to make sure we resolve it properly.
>
> [Agent Name]

### Step 2: Investigate

1. Pull order details and fulfillment history.
2. Check supplier batch records for the affected items.
3. Review delivery photos/logs (if available).
4. Check customer history — is this a repeat complaint?
5. Contact supplier if product quality issue — request their incident report.
6. Document all findings in the ticket.

### Step 3: Determine Resolution

| Complaint Type | Standard Resolution | Escalation Threshold |
|---------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Product Quality (minor) | Credit for affected item(s) | > $25 credit → Manager approval |
| Product Quality (health) | Full refund + credit + supplier investigation | Always escalate |
| Missing Items | Full refund for missing items or re-delivery | > $50 → Manager approval |
| Wrong Items | Full refund + correct items re-delivered | — |
| Late Delivery | Apology + delivery fee credit | > 4 hours late → additional goodwill credit |
| Damaged Packaging | Full refund for affected items | — |
| Pricing Dispute | Price match or refund difference | > $20 → Manager approval |
| App/Technical | Fix timeline + goodwill credit ($5-10) | Data loss → escalate to Engineering |
| Driver/Supplier Behavior | Apology + investigation + compensation | Any safety concern → escalate immediately |

### Step 4: Execute Resolution

1. Process refund/credit in the system.
2. If re-delivery: coordinate with logistics team.
3. If supplier issue: file supplier incident report.
4. Send resolution communication to customer (use script below).

**Resolution Script:**

> Hi [Customer Name],
>
> I've completed my review of your complaint about [issue]. Here's what I found and what I'm doing about it:
>
> **What happened:** [Brief explanation]
> **What we're doing:** [Specific resolution — refund amount, re-delivery, etc.]
> **When:** [Timeline for resolution]
>
> I've also [noted this with our supplier team / flagged this for our logistics review / reported this to engineering] to help prevent this from happening again.
>
> I'm sorry for the inconvenience. If there's anything else I can help with, please don't hesitate to reach out.
>
> [Agent Name]

### Step 5: Follow-Up

- **Low/Medium:** Follow-up email 48 hours after resolution
- **High:** Follow-up call within 24 hours
- **Critical:** Daily updates until fully resolved, then weekly check-ins for 30 days

### Step 6: Close & Document

- [ ] Confirm customer is satisfied with resolution
- [ ] Close ticket with resolution summary
- [ ] Log complaint in monthly metrics
- [ ] Update supplier scorecard if applicable
- [ ] Flag for process improvement if systemic issue

## Quality Standards

- Every customer should feel heard, not processed.
- Never blame the customer. Even if they're wrong, find a solution.
- Offer resolution proactively — don't make them ask for compensation.
- Document everything — if it's not in the ticket, it didn't happen.
- Close the loop — every complaint gets a follow-up, no exceptions.
