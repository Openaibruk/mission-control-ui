## 2. Current Strengths

### What They're Doing Well

1. **SGL Data Infrastructure Is Strong**
   - 260+ SGLs tracked with full history: start dates, order counts, kg volumes, total sales, status, top products
   - Top SGL "kira" has 12,963 orders / 35,328 kg / Birr 1,470,423 in lifetime sales
   - Clear active/inactive classification system
   - 10 SGLs with pickup hub contracts and guarantee documentation

2. **Survey Program Is Active & Sophisticated**
   - Running multiple concurrent surveys: delivery preference (~600 responses), packaging (1,378 responses), lower order cause, SGL demographics
   - Dedicated survey team with performance-based compensation (2,500 base + tiered per-call incentives)
   - Quality control system with random verification and call duration monitoring
   - 40 calls/day per agent target, 950+/month

3. **Competitive Intelligence Exists**
   - Benchmark sheet compares ChipChip prices vs 14 competitors across 18 products
   - Shows ChipChip is cheaper on core staples (red onion, tomato, potato) vs most competitors
   - Data reveals competitive weaknesses: garlic, ginger, chili pricing is uncompetitive vs some markets

4. **Conversion Funnel Is Well-Documented**
   - 9-phase SGL conversion flow from awareness → 30-day monitoring
   - Segmented approach: active/high-volume, active/low-volume, inactive/dormant SGLs
   - Level 1 (online) vs Level-Up (in-person) onboarding structure

5. **Content Strategy Has Clear Frameworks**
   - Delivery fee & pickup hub promotion with 3-week timeline
   - 6 design post ideas + 4 video concepts
   - Multi-channel approach: social media, SMS, in-app, group leader communication
   - Bilingual content (Amharic + English)

6. **Pickup Hub Project Is Detailed**
   - 14 identified hubs, 7 contracts signed
   - 2-week launch master plan with responsible parties and status tracking
   - Equipment costs calculated: Birr 9,100 per SGL / Birr 27,300 for 3
   - Training guidelines for both drivers and leaders

7. **SGL Complaint Tracking Is Transparent**
   - 3 periodic complaint reports (Feb 14-Mar 10)
   - Clear categorization: product quality, pricing, weight shortage, delivery delays
   - Company responses documented alongside SGL concerns
   - Specific issues tracked: carrot/red onion/potato quality, commission structure dissatisfaction

## 4. Data Opportunities (Untapped Insights)

1. **SGL Database = Gold Mine for Targeting**
   - 260 SGLs with lifetime order data, product preferences, activity status
   - Can identify: dormant high-potential SGLs, product preference patterns by area, seasonal trends
   - Top product categories: Potato, Red Onion, Tomato, White Cabbage, Carrot, Avocado

2. **Delivery Preference Survey = Pickup Hub Demand Map**
   - 600+ responses with location data
   - Can create geographic demand heatmap for hub placement
   - "Less than 300 meters" walk distance is the dominant preference

3. **Lower Order Cause Data = Retention Playbook**
   - Top 3 reasons SGLs reduce orders: PRICE, quality, delivery delay
   - Price is the #1 issue — competitive pricing on staple vegetables is critical
   - Commission reduction complaints specifically about tomato pricing

4. **SGL Demographics = Real Persona Data**
   - Majority are housewives ordering from home
   - Primary motivation: discount on products, extra income
   - Most can manage 5-10 people (small groups)
   - Biggest challenges: product quality, packaging, delivery delay

5. **Complaint Data = Product Quality Playbook**
   - Recurring issues: carrot (not fresh), red onion (small/dusty), potato (sprouted)
   - Weight shortages of 0.5-1kg are systemic
   - Commission structure dissatisfaction threatens SGL retention

6. **Benchmark Data = Pricing Strategy Foundation**
   - ChipChip is competitive on staples vs Sunday Market (cheaper by 3-12 birr)
   - But expensive vs Garment Market (cheaper by 5-8 birr on some items)
   - Garlic, ginger, avocado pricing is very uncompetitive vs specialized markets
