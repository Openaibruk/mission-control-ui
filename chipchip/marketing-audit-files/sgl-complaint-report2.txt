SGL Complaint Report
Reporting Period: February 23 – March 4
Sources:
* Verified Super Group Leaders Group
________________


1. Overview
During the reporting period, multiple complaints were raised by Super Group Leaders (SGLs). The issues are recurring and are affecting trust, operational performance, and financial outcomes for SGLs. The complaints mainly focus on product quality, stock management, commission calculation, system errors, and communication gaps.
________________


2. Major Complaint Categories
A. Damaged and Repacked Products
* SGLs reported receiving damaged products.
* When complaints are raised, products are sometimes repacked and resent instead of properly replaced.
* SGLs believe the issue may be coming from the Store or Procurement Department.
* There is concern that the problem is repeated intentionally and not treated seriously.
* SGLs stated this is not a “system error” but a recurring operational issue.
Impact:
* Loss of customer trust
* Conflict between SGLs and customers
* Financial loss and reputational damage
B. Quantity and Order Manipulation Concerns
* Example complaint:
Before 6:29 PM, product quantity showed availability for 12 people.
When SGLs tried to order, it changed to 10 people, then later to 3 people.
* SGLs feel confused and unfairly treated.
* They question what mistake they made when ordering based on displayed availability.
Concern:
SGLs believe the issue is not accidental and may involve internal mismanagement.
________________


C. Commission Structure and Financial Loss
SGLs raised serious concerns about commission differences:
   * In Super Group level:
Onion commission = 5 birr per kg
100 kg = 500 birr income
   * In current percentage system:
Example calculation:
39 birr × 100 kg = 3,900 birr
10% commission = 390 birr
This results in lower income compared to fixed commission.
SGLs stated:
      * Even 8% is not acceptable.
      * 10% still causes financial damage.
      * They have legal obligations and guarantees.
      * Small commission differences significantly affect their profit.
Impact:
      * Reduced motivation
      * Feeling of unfair treatment
      * Financial dissatisfaction
________________


D. Product Assortment Issues (Example: Diapers)
      * Size 2 diapers have low demand (mainly for newborn babies).
      * SGLs recommend:
      * If stocking Size 2, limit quantity (e.g., 10 packs only).
      * Increase stock of Size 3 and above, as demand is higher.
Concern:
Inventory planning does not match market demand.
________________


E. Delivery Delays (Example: Tomato Order)
      * The tomato order placed yesterday was not delivered.
      * SGLs were told it would arrive on time today.
      * Based on company assurance, SGLs informed their customers.
      * Delivery did not arrive as promised.
SGL Statement:
“We trusted you and informed our customers accordingly. Now our credibility is damaged.”
________________


F. Order Cancellation and Refund Issue
From company side explanation:
      * Yesterday’s order was canceled due to a system error.
      * All orders were automatically canceled.
      * Payment will be refunded to SGL wallet.
      * Tomato price today is 29 birr; SGLs can order today and receive tomorrow.
However, SGLs feel:
      * These responses are repeated frequently.
      * “System error” explanation is overused.
      * The issue is operational, not technical.
________________


G. Request for Urgent Meeting
One SGL strongly stated:
      * Before implementing agreements or operational changes, there must be broad discussion with SGLs.
      * Without structured communication, continuous conflict will occur.
      * There is fear that disputes may escalate legally.
      * An urgent meeting is requested.
________________


3. Key Risks Identified
      1. Loss of trust between SGLs and company
      2. Damage to SGL-customer relationships
      3. Reduced motivation among SGLs
      4. Financial dissatisfaction
      5. Potential legal conflict
      6. Reputation risk for the company
________________


Official Response from ChipChip Side
The company acknowledges the seriousness of the concerns raised by SGLs.
Response Statement:
      * We understand that the situation that occurred is frustrating and unacceptable.
      * We sincerely apologize for the inconvenience caused.
      * We are working internally to ensure that such issues do not happen again.
      * Today's remaining orders will be delivered within the remaining working hours.
      * Yesterday’s canceled orders were due to a system error; all canceled payments will be refunded to your wallet.
      * Today’s tomato price is 29 birr; orders placed today will be delivered tomorrow.
      * We are reviewing store, procurement, and system processes to prevent recurrence.
      * An urgent discussion meeting with SGL representatives will be arranged to address concerns transparently and align expectations.
4. Recommended Actions
      1. Conduct urgent SGL meeting
      2. Review commission structure fairness
      3. Audit store and procurement process
      4. Improve system transparency on stock quantities
      5. Improve demand-based inventory planning
      6. Strengthen communication before operational changes
