Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)
Driver Negligence Prevention – Delivery Driver & Assistant


1. Purpose
The purpose of this SOP is to prevent driver and assistant negligence during delivery operations by clearly defining responsibilities, standards, and controls across the full delivery cycle—from arrival time and item picking to delivery conduct, customer handling, and post‑delivery vehicle checks.


2. Scope
This SOP applies to all delivery drivers and delivery assistants involved in:
* Order pickup and loading
* Transportation and delivery
* Customer interaction
* Handling of goods before, during, and after delivery


3. Definitions
* Negligence: Failure to follow assigned duties, SOPs, or reasonable care, resulting in delays, loss, damage, or customer complaints.
* Driver: Person responsible for operating the delivery vehicle.
* Assistant: Person supporting the driver in loading, checking, delivery, and customer coordination.






4. Roles and Responsibilities
4.1 Driver Responsibilities
* Arrive on time at pickup and delivery locations
* Ensure safe driving and adherence to routes
* Verify all items are loaded and delivered
* Maintain professional behavior at all times
* Conduct vehicle checks before and after delivery
4.2 Assistant Responsibilities
* Support item verification and loading
* Ensure no order or item is left behind
* Assist in customer communication and delivery confirmation
* Report any issue immediately to the driver and supervisor


5. SOP Procedures
5.1 Arrival Time & Punctuality
* Drivers and assistants must arrive at the pickup location at or before the scheduled time
* Any expected delay must be reported immediately to the supervisor
* Late arrival without valid reason is considered negligence
* Arrival time must be recorded (manual log or system)
Negligence Examples:
* Late arrival without notification
* Leaving the pickup location without authorization


5.2 Picking & Loading Items (No Order Left Behind)
* Match items strictly against the delivery order or invoice
* Count items carefully before loading
* Cross‑check order quantity, type, and packaging condition
* Driver and assistant must both confirm all items are loaded
* Partial loading without approval is strictly prohibited
Negligence Examples:
* Missing items during pickup
* Loading wrong items or quantities
* Failing to verify orders


5.3 Delivery Execution
* Follow the assigned delivery route unless instructed otherwise
* Deliver items only to the correct customer/location
* Do not leave items unattended unless authorized
* Protect items from damage, theft, or weather exposure
* Insert the delivery code immediately upon successful delivery[a]
* Do not return any order without confirmation from both the customer and Customer Service
* Any return must be clearly approved and recorded before leaving the delivery location
Negligence Examples:
* Delivering to the wrong customer
* Leaving goods outside without permission
* Mishandling fragile or sensitive items
* Failing to insert delivery code on time
* Returning orders without proper confirmation


5.4 Delivery Behavior & Professional Conduct
* Wear appropriate attire and maintain clean appearance
* Speak respectfully and professionally
* Avoid arguments or inappropriate language
* Do not request tips, favors, or personal benefits
* Follow company rules and local regulations
Negligence Examples:
* Rude or aggressive behavior
* Unprofessional appearance
* Ignoring customer concerns




5.5 Customer Handling & Communication
* Greet customers politely and confirm order details
* Allow the customer to inspect items when required
* Obtain delivery confirmation (signature, photo, or system confirmation)
* Report any customer complaint immediately
* Never argue with customers; escalate issues to management
Negligence Examples:
* Failure to confirm delivery
* Poor communication with customers
* Ignoring customer complaints


5.6 Items Left in Vehicle (Post‑Delivery Check)
* After each delivery, the driver and assistant must check the vehicle
* Ensure no items, packages, or documents remain inside
* Conduct a final vehicle inspection at end of shift
* Any discovered leftover item must be reported immediately
Negligence Examples:
* Leaving customer items in the vehicle
* Delayed reporting of leftover items


6. Incident Reporting
* All delays, damages, missing items, or complaints must be reported immediately
* Reports must include time, location, reason, and persons involved
* Failure to report incidents is considered negligence


7. Disciplinary Actions
Failure to comply with this SOP will result in penalties and disciplinary measures as outlined below. All penalties are applied per incident.


7.1 Penalties[b]
1. Missing Item: Driver will pay 2× the item value[c]
2. Item Left in Vehicle or Warehouse: Driver will be penalized 500 Birr (if the item is still in company possession)
3. Bad or Unprofessional Behavior: Driver will pay 700 Birr
4. Unauthorized / Unknowing Return: Driver will pay 500 Birr
5. Delivery Code Issue: Driver will pay the full price of the returned item
6. Failure to Reach Customer on Time:
   * If delay is unavoidable and not under driver control → No penalty
   * If driver is able to deliver but returns the order without valid reason → 500 Birr penalty[d]
7.2 Progressive Disciplinary Process
* 1st Penalty: Verbal Warning
* 2nd Penalty: Written Warning
* 3rd Penalty: Termination of contract or employment


8. Key Control & Prevention Points
* Always double‑check orders (pickup & delivery)
* Clear communication between driver and assistant
* Strict adherence to time schedules
* Mandatory vehicle checks after delivery
* Immediate issue reporting—no delay, no hiding
