Official Communication Policy
Mandatory for all staff, contractors, and collaborators.

ChipChip operates with clear ownership of tools.
If information is not in the right place, it does not exist.
Telegram: Execution & alerts
Calendar: Time & commitments
Documents: Decisions & records
Dashboards: Numbers & performance
1. Purpose: One Source of Truth

This document aims to:
All work-related communication must follow this policy.
Reduce noise and confusion
Protect decision authority
Ensure clarity, accountability, and speed
Align all teams with a single execution system
Goal of the Policy

One Purpose
Every channel exists for a specific reason.
Authority First
Decisions follow the org structure.
Facts Only
Execution channels are factual and concise.
Accountability
Decisions must include owner and deadline.
No Shadow Ops
Decisions outside official channels are invalid.
Alignment
We move as one team.

3.1 Telegram: Execution, Not Thinking

Rules:
The 60-Second Rule
If a message cannot be understood and acted upon in under 60 seconds, it does not belong in Telegram.
Post only in the correct group
Follow group description and target
No cross-posting screenshots
No voice notes for decisions
Telegram Discipline

Single Source of Truth for:
KPIs and metrics
Financial tracking (CP1 / CP2 / cash)
Operational logs and registers
Planning tables
Rule: One owner per file. No parallel versions. Telegram must reference the sheet link.
3.2 Excel / Sheets: System of Record

Use For:
Rules:
External partners, vendors, banks
Legal or compliance
Formal approvals
Internal execution must not rely on email
Email is not a substitute for Telegram
3.3 Email: External & Formal

Every decision shared must include 3 elements. If any are missing, the decision is invalid.
What is decided
Who owns it
By when it must be executed
4.1 Mandatory Decision Format

Skipping levels creates confusion and will be corrected.
4.2 Escalation Path

Strictly Prohibited:
Operational debates in announcement groups
Decisions made in private chats
Re-opening closed decisions without new data
Using multiple tools for the same purpose
Emotional or accusatory language
5. What Is Not Allowed

Everyone is responsible for:
Managers: Must enforce this policy.Repeated violations will be treated as execution issues.
Using the correct channel
Respecting decision authority
Keeping communication clear and professional
6. Accountability

Teams may create groups for focused execution (e.g., rollout, pilot, incident).
Rules:
Must have a single purpose & end condition
Must be owned by a team lead
Must reference an official core group as parent
Must be archived when completed
8. Temporary Channels

The Admin (Lead) is responsible for:
Enforcing the group purpose
Redirecting off-topic messages
Ensuring outputs are produced
If a group loses clarity, the admin must intervene immediately.
