INTERNAL PROCESS & ACCOUNTABILITY AGREEMENT
Meki Supplier Consignment Pilot – ChipChip
Effective Date: __________
Pilot Duration: __________
________________


1. Objective
To define clear operational ownership, accountability, and financial responsibility for the Meki Tomato Consignment Model.
All departments agree that:
* Inventory belongs to Supplier until sold.
* Damage must be categorized accurately.
* No damage may remain “unassigned”.
* All communication must be documented.
________________


2. Process Flow & Department Ownership
________________


STAGE 1 — Price Notification & Bidding
Owner: App Team
Support: Sourcing Team
Process:
1. Supplier sends:
   * Quantity
   * Grade expectation
   * Base price
   * Dispatch timing
2. App Team:
   * Logs price
   * Benchmarks against:
      * Sunday market
      * Local shop prices
      * ChipChip historical data
3. Sourcing Team:
   * Compares with other suppliers
   * Confirms winning supplier
No dispatch without confirmation.
Failure in benchmarking or incorrect price approval → App Team & Sourcing accountable.
________________


STAGE 2 — Transport to Warehouse
Owner: Supplier
Internal Monitoring: Warehouse
Supplier responsible for transport until warehouse arrival.
Warehouse must:
* Log arrival time
* Record visible transport damage
If internal assistance in transport is provided:
→ No liability transfer unless agreed in writing.
________________


STAGE 3 — Arrival & QC Grading
Owner: QC Team
Oversight: Warehouse Supervisor
QC must:
* Record total received KG
* Record arrival damage %
* Split into:
   * Grade A
   * Grade B
   * Edible Damage
   * Waste
* Upload photos
* Declare shelf life (24hr / 48hr)
* Communicate grading completion timeline
If grading delay occurs without notification and damage increases:
→ QC accountable.
Arrival damage must be frozen and cannot be modified later.
________________


STAGE 4 — Inventory & Stock Control
Owner: Warehouse
Warehouse must:
* Physically separate consignment stock
* Apply FIFO
* Track batch ID
* Maintain stock accuracy
If stock mismatch causes unpicked orders:
→ Warehouse accountable.
________________


STAGE 5 — Pricing & Run Rate Management
Owner: App Team
App Team must:
* Monitor daily run rate
* Compare with:
   * Sunday market
   * Local shop price
   * ChipChip trends
* Notify supplier immediately if movement is slow
* Document notification timestamp
If supplier refuses price adjustment:
→ Supplier carries expiry risk.
If App Team fails to notify and spoilage occurs:
→ App Team accountable.
________________


STAGE 6 — Marketing
Owner: Marketing Team
Responsibilities:
* SMS campaigns
* Push notifications
* Banner placements
* Store promotion
Marketing supports sales but does not guarantee volume.
Failure to execute approved campaign → Marketing accountable internally.
________________


STAGE 7 — Order Processing & Fulfillment
Owner: Warehouse
Support: QC
Warehouse must:
* Pick correct batch
* Check visual quality before dispatch
* Avoid sending damaged products
If damaged product sent to customer:
→ Warehouse accountable for compensation.
________________


STAGE 8 — Last-Mile Delivery
Owner: Logistics
Logistics must:
* Deliver on time
* Handle goods carefully
* Record proof of delivery code and quantity and quality approval at the spot
If product damaged during delivery:
→ Logistics accountable.
________________


STAGE 9 — Returns Handling
Owner: QC
Support: Warehouse
All returns must be inspected immediately.
QC must:
1. Record batch number
2. Record reason code
3. Inspect condition
4. Categorize:
   * Resellable
   * Edible
   * Waste
5. Assign accountability
No returned product re-enters inventory without QC tagging.
________________

STAGE 10 — Financial Processing & Settlement
Owner: App Team
Support: Finance
App Team must:
* Close batch after selling window
* Generate batch report:
   * Opening stock
   * Sold
   * Return damage
   * Expiry
   * Margin
* Send settlement report to supplier
Customers pay upfront.
Settlement occurs after delivery completion and batch closure.
Failure in reporting accuracy → App Team accountable.
________________


3. Damage Categories (Mandatory Separation)
Each batch must track separately:
1. Arrival Damage
2. Edible Discount
3. Warehouse Damage
4. Fulfillment Damage
5. Last-Mile Damage
6. Expiry – Supplier Pricing Refusal
7. Expiry – Operational Failure
Mixing categories is prohibited.
________________


4. Financial Accountability Rule
If department failure causes:
* Unpicked orders
* Spoilage
* Emergency delivery cost
* Customer compensation
* Extra KG compensation
The responsible department head must report incident and accept internal accountability.
________________
