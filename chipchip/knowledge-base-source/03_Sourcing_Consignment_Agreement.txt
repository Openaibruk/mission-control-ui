1. Business Model
1.1 Model Type
This arrangement is a Consignment Marketplace Model.
* Inventory ownership remains with the Supplier until sold to the final customer.
* ChipChip provides:
   * App store profile
   * Marketing support
   * Warehousing
   * Fulfillment
   * Delivery
   * Settlement processing
________________


2. Store Profile & Marketing
2.1 Dedicated Store
ChipChip will create a separate store profile under Supplier name.
Products will be listed under this store.
________________


2.2 Marketing Support
ChipChip Marketing Team will support by:
* SMS campaigns
* Push notifications
* In-app banners
* Store promotion
* Customer communication
Marketing does not guarantee sales volume but aims to improve visibility and run rate.
________________


3. Pre-Dispatch & Bidding Process
3.1 Price Notification
Before dispatch, Supplier must notify App Team:
* Quantity (KG)
* Grade expectation
* Base price (e.g., 26 birr/kg)
* Expected dispatch date
________________


3.2 Competitive Review
App Team will:
* Submit Supplier price into Sourcing comparison
* Benchmark against:
   * Other suppliers
   * Sunday market price
   * Local shop prices
   * ChipChip historical trends
If Supplier wins:
→ Dispatch confirmed.
If Supplier loses:
Options:
1. Request price discount
2. Supply product from alternative supplier and allow Meki Supplier to resell via store
No shipment without price approval confirmation.
________________


4. Transport to Warehouse
* Supplier responsible for transport to Addis warehouse.
* Transport damage risk: Supplier.
* ChipChip may assist with transport contacts but holds no transport liability.
________________


5. Arrival, Grading & Shelf Life
5.1 Intake Recording
Upon arrival, QC will record:
* Total KG received
* Arrival damage %
* Grade breakdown:
   * Grade A
   * Grade B
   * Edible Damage
   * Waste (non-sellable)
Arrival damage = Supplier responsibility.
________________


5.2 Edible Damage
Edible damage:
* Slight cosmetic or softness defect
* Sold at discount
* Must include:
   * Real product image
   * Description from QC
Revenue remains Supplier’s (minus margin).
________________


5.3 Shelf Life Declaration
QC will declare:
* Selling window (24 or 48 hours)
* Grading completion timeline
If grading cannot be completed same day:
* Timeline must be communicated
* Progress updated in system
If operational delay without communication causes spoilage:
→ ChipChip may bear responsibility.
________________


6. Pricing & Margin
6.1 Selling Price Formula
Supplier Base Price: e.g., 26 birr/kg
ChipChip adds 5–10% margin on top.
Example:
26 birr + 10% = 28.6 birr selling price.
________________


6.2 Market Monitoring
App Team will monitor:
* Sunday market price
* Local shop price
* ChipChip trends
* Run rate
If slow movement:
→ Supplier notified immediately.
If Supplier refuses adjustment and spoilage occurs:
→ Supplier bears loss.
If ChipChip fails to notify:
→ ChipChip may share/absorb loss.
All communication documented.
________________


7. Order & Delivery Model
7.1 Prepaid Orders
* Customers pay first.
* Delivery happens after payment.
* Settlement occurs only after delivery completion and batch closure.
________________


7.2 Return Scenarios
Returns may occur due to:
* Customer postponement
* Wrong location
* Customer unreachable
* Refusal
Returned products go directly to QC for inspection.
________________


7.3 Return Inspection
QC will:
* Inspect immediately
* Categorize:
   * Re-sellable
   * Edible discount
   * Waste
* Record:
   * Batch number
   * Customer ID
   * Driver ID
   * Damage %
________________

10. Batch-Based Settlement
Each shipment is treated as a Batch.
Settlement occurs when:
* Batch sold out OR
* Selling window expired
Settlement calculation:
Total Sold KG × Selling Price
– ChipChip Margin (5–10%)
= Net Payable to Supplier
Inventory not sold remains Supplier property.
Payment Term: __________
