# Standard Operating Procedure: Supplier/Vendor Onboarding

**Document ID:** CHIP-OPS-002
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2026-03-16
**Owner:** Supplier Relations Team
**Review Cycle:** Quarterly

---

## 1. Purpose

To establish a structured, repeatable process for onboarding new suppliers and vendors onto the ChipChip platform, ensuring quality, compliance, and operational readiness.

## 2. Scope

All new suppliers, farms, distributors, and vendors who will list products or fulfill orders through ChipChip.

## 3. Onboarding Pipeline

### Stage 1: Sourcing & Initial Contact (Days 1-3)

1. **Identify candidate** through market research, referral programs, or inbound inquiries.
2. Supplier Relations Associate conducts initial screening call:
   - Product range and categories
   - Production capacity (daily/weekly volume)
   - Geographic coverage area
   - Existing customer base and distribution channels
3. Complete the **Supplier Interest Form** (see Appendix A).
4. Decision to proceed → move to Stage 2. Decline → send polite rejection with feedback.

### Stage 2: Documentation & Compliance (Days 4-10)

Collect and verify the following:

- [ ] Business registration / trade license
- [ ] Tax identification number
- [ ] Food handling / safety certifications (where applicable)
- [ ] Insurance certificates (liability, product)
- [ ] Bank account details for payment processing
- [ ] Product catalog with pricing (wholesale rates)
- [ ] Sample products for quality evaluation

**Compliance Checks:**
1. Verify business registration with relevant government database.
2. Confirm food safety certifications are current and valid.
3. Run basic financial health check (for suppliers handling >$5K/month).
4. Check for any regulatory violations or complaints on public record.

### Stage 3: Quality Evaluation (Days 8-14)

1. Request product samples (minimum 3 SKUs from their top categories).
2. QA team evaluates against ChipChip standards:
   - Freshness and appearance
   - Packaging quality
   - Shelf life adequacy
   - Taste/texture (food items)
   - Labeling accuracy (ingredients, origin, weight)
3. Score on **1-5 scale** across each criterion.
4. Minimum passing score: **3.5 average, no single criterion below 3.0**.
5. Failed evaluation → provide specific feedback and allow one re-submission within 14 days.

### Stage 4: Agreement & Setup (Days 12-18)

1. Send **Supplier Partnership Agreement** covering:
   - Pricing and payment terms (Net 15 / Net 30)
   - Minimum order quantities
   - Delivery/pickup responsibilities
   - Return and dispute resolution terms
   - Data sharing and confidentiality
   - Termination conditions
2. Supplier signs agreement (digital signature accepted).
3. Set up supplier in the platform:
   - Create supplier account
   - Configure product listings
   - Set delivery zones and availability
   - Set up payment profile
4. Assign a dedicated **Supplier Success Manager**.

### Stage 5: Training & Go-Live (Days 16-21)

1. **Mandatory training session** (2 hours, virtual or in-person):
   - Platform dashboard walkthrough
   - Order management and fulfillment process
   - Quality standards and packaging guidelines
   - Communication protocols (Slack channel, support hotline)
   - Reporting and analytics access
2. Supplier completes training quiz (minimum 80% score).
3. **Soft launch**: Supplier receives their first 5 orders with coaching support.
4. After 5 successful orders → supplier status set to `ACTIVE`.
5. 30-day check-in call scheduled with Supplier Success Manager.

## 5. Red Flags — Automatic Disqualification

- Missing or expired food safety certifications
- History of product recalls in the past 12 months
- Unwillingness to provide product samples
- Negative references from existing business partners
- Inability to meet minimum quality standards after re-submission

## 6. Rejection Process

1. Send formal rejection email with specific reasons.
2. Log rejection reason in supplier CRM.
3. If fixable issues: invite re-application after 90 days.
4. If fundamental misalignment: note "Do Not Re-engage" in CRM.
