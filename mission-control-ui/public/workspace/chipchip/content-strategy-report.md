## 1. Company Overview

### What is ChipChip?

ChipChip is an Ethiopian food supply chain platform that connects farmers, suppliers, restaurants, and consumers through a digitally orchestrated network. The company is transitioning from a traditional warehouse-based model to an **asset-light orchestration model**—controlling flows digitally rather than physically handling products.

**Mission:** "ChipChip stops touching products and starts controlling flows."

**Revenue Model:**
- Trade margin (50%)
- Financing/BNPL services (35%)
- Supplier tools & ads (15%)

**Current Financial Snapshot (February 2026):**
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Monthly Sales | 7.4M ETB |
| Gross Margin | -16.68% (improved from -45.49% in January) |
| Monthly Burn Rate | 10.16M ETB |
| Total Orders | 83,731 |
| Total Volume | 153,989 kg |

**Key Strategic Shift:** Moving from "owning flow, holding stock, repacking" to "designing flow, enforcing rules, orchestrating suppliers → hubs → customers."

## 2. Target Audience

Based on the knowledge base, ChipChip serves **four distinct customer segments**:

### Segment 1: Individual Consumers (B2C)
- **Volume:** 70.9% of total volume, 72.4% of revenue
- **Order Size:** ~2.14 kg/order, 88 ETB/order
- **Channel Share:** 85.7% of orders
- **Pain Points:** High delivery fees (150 ETB), unreliable supply, price volatility
- **Strategy:** Migration to Super Leader pickup points

### Segment 2: Super Leaders (Community Aggregators)
- **Volume:** 15.5% of total volume, 11.6% of revenue
- **Tier:** 30kg+ orders
- **Role:** Community drop-off points, micro-sorting, local distribution
- **Incentive:** 5% GMV commission after confirmed pickup
- **Growth Driver:** Driver-led recruitment with milestone incentives

### Segment 3: Restaurants & HORECA (B2B)
- **Volume:** 11% of total volume, 15.8% of revenue
- **Tier:** 100kg+ orders
- **Pain Points:** Reliability, credit access, supply consistency
- **Strategy:** Strong contracts, credit-inclusive pricing, BNPL

### Segment 4: B2B Captains (Restaurant Aggregators)
- **Role:** Collect weekly restaurant demand, recurring orders
- **Tier:** Restaurant-level aggregation

## 3. Content Pillars

Based on the knowledge base analysis, the Super Group content strategy should focus on **5 core pillars**:

### Pillar 1: Pricing as Discipline
**Key Message:** "ChipChip prices demand into discipline—not persuades it."

**Content Angles:**
- Explain the gate pricing model (Individual +25% margin vs Group Member ≥15% discount)
- Show cost savings comparison tables
- Educate on fee structures (150 ETB individual → 10 ETB group member → FREE at 50kg+)
- Highlight volume-based incentives

### Pillar 2: Super Leader Opportunity
**Key Message:** "Earn income by aggregating your community's demand."

**Content Angles:**
- Super Leader success stories / testimonials
- Step-by-step joining process
- Commission structure explained (5% GMV)
- Weekly commitment windows and how they work
- Driver partnership stories (vehicle upgrade journeys)

### Pillar 3: Restaurant & B2B Reliability
**Key Message:** "Reliable supply + cash flow flexibility for your restaurant."

**Content Angles:**
- How ChipChip ensures SLA compliance
- BNPL explainer (7-14 day cycles)
- Approved supplier network quality assurance
- Cutoff time benefits (7PM order, next morning delivery)
- Contract-based pricing with credit built in

### Pillar 4: Asset-Light Innovation
**Key Message:** "Technology-first supply chain. No warehouse. No middlemen. Better prices."

**Content Angles:**
- Explain the orchestration model (Farm → Supplier DC → Super Leader → Customer)
- Digital order aggregation
- Real-time tracking
- Blockchain trade credit ledger (future vision)
- Data-driven demand intelligence

### Pillar 5: Community & Trust
**Key Message:** "Affordable groceries with public trust."

**Content Angles:**
- Open supply chain visibility
- Supplier reputation network
- Price benchmarking transparency
- Quality assurance processes
- Return and refund policies (crate/bag system)
