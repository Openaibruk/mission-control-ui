# ChipChip Marketing Content Strategy & Editorial Calendar

**Prepared by:** Marketing Team (Nova + Nahom)

**Version:** 1.0

## 1. Executive Summary

ChipChip is Ethiopia's technology-first food supply chain platform, connecting farmers, suppliers, restaurants, and consumers through a digitally orchestrated network. Our content strategy transforms how ChipChip communicates: from sporadic posts to a systematic, data-driven content engine that builds community, drives adoption, and positions ChipChip as the definitive food-tech brand in East Africa.

**Core message:** "We don't touch products. We control flows."

---

## 2. Brand Voice & Personality

| Attribute | We Are | We Are Not |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Tone | Friendly, casual, energetic | Corporate, stiff, salesy |
| Language | Simple, everyday Amharic + English | Jargon-heavy, overly formal |
| Attitude | Community-first neighbor | Distant corporation |
| Humor | Light, food-pun friendly | Sarcastic, edgy |

### Key Phrases
- "Fresh from [farm/supplier] to your door"
- "Save more when you buy together"
- "Your neighborhood's group buy"
- "Quality you can taste, prices you'll love"
- "Order by 7PM, delivered by morning"

### Phrases to Avoid
- "Cheapest prices" (compete on value, not just price)
- "Cut out the middleman" (we ARE the platform)
- Unsubstantiated health claims

## 3. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: 🛒 Product & Pricing (25%)
*Show the value, explain the savings*

- Price comparisons (individual vs group buying)
- Delivery fee breakdowns (150 ETB individual → FREE at 50kg+)
- New product announcements
- Seasonal availability alerts
- "This week's best deals" posts

### Pillar 2: 👥 Community & SGL Spotlight (25%)
*Celebrate the people who make it work*

- Super Leader success stories
- Community pickup point highlights
- Customer testimonials
- "SGL of the Week" features
- Group buy milestones (e.g., "Kirkos SGLs ordered 500kg this week!")

### Pillar 3: 🍳 Food & Recipes (20%)
*Value-add content that keeps people coming back*

- Simple Ethiopian recipes using common order items
- Seasonal cooking guides
- Food storage tips (reducing waste)
- Restaurant partner recipe features
- "What to cook this week" series

### Pillar 4: 🏢 Behind the Scenes (15%)
*Build trust through transparency*

- Supplier visits and quality checks
- How the delivery route works (visual)
- Team introductions
- Day-in-the-life of a driver/SGL
- Partnership announcements

### Pillar 5: 📊 Education & How-To (15%)
*Empower users to get the most out of ChipChip*

- How to join a Super Group
- How to become a Super Leader
- App walkthrough (screenshots/video)
- Restaurant onboarding steps
- "Did you know?" series about supply chain

## 4. Channel Strategy

### Facebook (Primary — 5 posts/week)
| Day | Content Type | Pillar |
|-----|-------------|--------|
| Monday | "Week's Best Deals" | Product |
| Tuesday | SGL/Community Spotlight | Community |
| Wednesday | Recipe post | Food |
| Thursday | Behind the scenes | BTS |
| Friday | Engagement post (poll/question) | Community |

**Format:** Longer captions, carousel images, community discussion focus  
**Groups:** Maintain neighborhood-specific buy groups  
**Boost:** Top 2 posts/week (500 ETB each for reach)

### Instagram (4 posts/week + daily Stories)
| Day | Feed Post | Stories |
|-----|-----------|---------|
| Mon | Product showcase (beautiful food photo) | Deal of the day |
| Tue | SGL feature / testimonial | "How it works" highlight |
| Wed | Recipe Reel (60s) | Poll: "What should we stock next?" |
| Thu | Behind the scenes | Team spotlight |

**Format:** Visual-first, Reels for reach, Stories for engagement  
**Highlights:** How to Order | SGL Program | Recipes | Reviews

### Telegram (Daily — operational channel)
- Daily deals and flash stock alerts
- SGL group coordination messages
- Order confirmations and delivery updates
- Quick polls for product demand

### TikTok (3 videos/week)
- **Monday:** "Day in the life" — driver, SGL, or restaurant partner
- **Wednesday:** Price comparison challenge ("500 ETB haul")
- **Friday:** Recipe or food prep with trending audio

**Format:** 30-60s, vertical, Amharic with English captions for diaspora reach

### WhatsApp/SMS (Targeted — weekly)
- SGL-specific: group order reminders, commission updates
- Restaurant partners: weekly specials, BNPL reminders
- New customer welcome sequences (3-message drip)

## 7. User-Generated Content (UGC) Strategy

### Collect
- **#MyChipChipHaul** campaign — weekly photo contest
- **SGL testimonials** — monthly video request
- **Restaurant partner stories** — quarterly feature
- **Delivery driver diaries** — weekly behind-the-scenes

### Amplify
- Repost best UGC on all channels (with credit)
- Create "Community Corner" monthly highlight
- Feature UGC in paid ads (authentic > polished)

### Incentivize
- Best monthly haul → 500 ETB credit
- Best SGL story → featured + bonus commission
- Best recipe using ChipChip ingredients → featured + credit

## 8. Content Production Workflow

### Weekly Cycle
| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| Monday | Content planning + scheduling for the week |
| Tuesday-Thursday | Content creation (copy, images, video) |
| Friday | Review + approve + schedule next week |
| Weekend | Monitor engagement, respond to comments |

### Approval Process
1. Content creator drafts → 2. Marketing lead reviews → 3. Brief team if needed → 4. Schedule/publish

### Tools Needed
- **Canva** — graphics templates (brand kit)
- **Meta Business Suite** — Facebook/Instagram scheduling
- **Telegram Bot** — automated group messages
- **Google Sheets** — content calendar tracking

## 9. KPIs & Measurement

### Monthly Metrics

| Metric | Facebook | Instagram | TikTok | Telegram |
|--------|----------|-----------|--------|----------|

| Link clicks | 500 | 300 | 200 | 1K |

### Business Metrics
- **SGL signups from content** → target: 20/month
- **Restaurant signups from content** → target: 10/month  
- **Order volume from social** → track via referral codes
- **Cost per acquisition** from paid content

### Weekly Review
Every Monday, review:
1. Top 3 performing posts (why they worked)
2. Bottom 3 (why they didn't)
3. Engagement patterns (what time works best)
4. Competitor content moves
